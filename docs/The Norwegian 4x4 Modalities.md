# Norwegian 4x4: Modality Guide

Use these specific setups to ensure you hit the required intensity (85–95% Max HR) across different types of equipment.

---

### 🏃 1. Treadmill (Precision Control)
*   **Setup:** Set a constant **incline of 3% to 5%**. This forces the heart rate up without requiring "sprint" speeds that can be dangerous or hard on joints.
*   **The Work (4m):** Increase speed until you are at a "heavy pant." You should be able to maintain this speed for the full 4 minutes without grabbing the rails.
*   **The Rest (3m):** Reduce speed to a slow walk (2.5–3.0 mph). **Keep the incline up** to make the transition back to work easier.

### 🌲 2. Outdoor Running (The Natural Way)
*   **Setup:** Find a **long, gentle hill** (approx. 4–6% grade). If no hills are available, use a flat, unobstructed path.
*   **The Work (4m):** Run at a "hard-sustainable" pace. It should feel like a race pace you could only hold for about 10–12 minutes total.
*   **The Rest (3m):** Turn around and walk/jog slowly back down the hill. Movement must be continuous.

### 🚣 3. Rowing Machine (Full Body)
*   **Setup:** Set the damper (drag) to **4 or 6**. High drag (10) usually leads to muscle failure before heart failure.
*   **The Work (4m):** Focus on a powerful leg drive. Keep your Stroke Rate (SPM) between **24 and 28**. 
*   **The Rest (3m):** Keep the handle moving with very light tension. Focus on deep, rhythmic belly breaths.

### 🚲 4. Stationary or Air Bike (Low Impact)
*   **Setup:** Adjust the seat height so your leg is almost straight at the bottom of the pedal stroke.
*   **The Work (4m):** Maintain a high, consistent RPM (usually 80+ on a road bike or 60+ on an Air/Assault bike). Use your arms on an Air Bike to share the load.
*   **The Rest (3m):** Pedal very slowly with zero resistance. Do not stop moving your legs.

### 🧗 5. Stair Climber (Vertical Power)
*   **Setup:** Stand upright. Do not lean your weight onto the side handles.
*   **The Work (4m):** Increase the "Level" or "Speed" until you cannot breathe through your nose. Take consistent, full steps rather than short, choppy ones.
*   **The Rest (3m):** Drop the machine to Level 1 or 2. Focus on standing tall to open up your lungs.

---

## 🛑 The "Golden Rule" for All Methods
If you cannot complete the 4th minute of the 4th set, your intensity was **too high**. If you aren't breathing heavily by the end of the 2nd minute of any set, your intensity is **too low**.
