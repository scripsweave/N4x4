Origin

Born in Trondheim

In 2007, researchers at the Norwegian University of Science and Technology showed that four four-minute intervals at 85–95% of max heart rate raise VO₂ max more than steady training. Fire ganger fire. Four by four. It needs no gym, no gear, and barely half an hour.

The Protocol
01

Warm up

5 minutes · easy pace

Ease the engine up to temperature. Five easy minutes lift your heart rate gently, deepen your breathing, and make sure the first interval doesn't arrive as a shock.

02

Go hard

4 minutes · 85–95% max HR

Four minutes past the edge of conversation — hard enough that you couldn't hold one. N4x4 watches your live heart rate and tells you to push or ease off. Your only job is to keep moving.

03

Recover

3 minutes · stay moving

Three minutes of easy movement. Recovery isn't a pause in the training — it is the training. Let the heart settle so the next effort counts as much as the first.

04

Repeat ×4

then 3 minutes cool-down

Four efforts, three recoveries, one easy cool-down home. Thirty-three minutes after you started, you're done — and you don't owe anyone anything until next week.

The founding study

Helgerud et al., 2007 — Med Sci Sports Exerc. Four 4-minute intervals at 90–95% of max heart rate improved VO₂ max significantly more than moderate continuous training at matched total work.

The mortality data

Mandsager et al., 2018 — JAMA Network Open, 122,007 patients. Higher cardiorespiratory fitness was associated with lower all-cause mortality, with no upper limit of benefit — roughly a five-fold gap between the least fit and elite.
