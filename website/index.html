WHY VO2 MAX

The fitness marker most
tied to how you age.

"Cardiorespiratory fitness, as measured by VO2 max, is the single greatest predictor of lifespan — more than smoking, more than diabetes, more than heart disease."

— Dr. Peter Attia, MD · Author of Outlive (his view; not affiliated with N4x4)
📉

It declines with age

VO2 max tends to drop roughly 10% per decade after your mid-20s without targeted training — but focused intervals are one of the most effective ways to push back.

🫀

Fitness tracks with health

In a study of 122,000 patients, the least-fit quartile had a higher mortality risk than smoking, hypertension, or diabetes. Association, not a guarantee — but a strong signal.

🔬

Intervals raise it efficiently

High-intensity intervals are among the most efficient ways to raise VO2 max, more so than steady-state cardio. The Norwegian 4x4 is one of the most studied protocols for it.

*Sources: Mandsager et al., JAMA Network Open, 2018 (fitness & mortality, 122,007 patients) · Helgerud et al., 2007 (the 4×4 protocol). Cardiorespiratory fitness is associated with long-term health; it is not a promise of any individual outcome.

WHAT HAPPENS IN YOUR BODY

Every session triggers
a cascade of adaptations.

🔥 EPOC Effect

You keep burning calories for hours

High-intensity intervals create excess post-exercise oxygen consumption (EPOC). Your metabolism stays elevated for up to 24 hours after the session ends.

⚡ Mitochondrial Biogenesis

Your cells get more powerful

HIIT is a strong trigger for mitochondrial biogenesis — your muscle cells build more mitochondria. More mitochondria means more energy and better endurance.

🩸 VEGF Response

You grow new blood vessels

Intense intervals spike VEGF (vascular endothelial growth factor), stimulating angiogenesis — the growth of new capillaries. Better circulation means better oxygen delivery to every organ.

🧠 BDNF Release

Your brain gets stronger too

High-intensity cardio raises BDNF — brain-derived neurotrophic factor, sometimes nicknamed "fertiliser for the brain." It's associated in research with better memory and focus.

💪 Stroke Volume

Your heart pumps more per beat

Endurance training increases stroke volume — the blood your heart moves with each beat. In one two-year study, structured exercise improved heart elasticity in previously sedentary middle-aged adults.

🫁 Oxygen Extraction

Your muscles use oxygen better

Repeated high-intensity intervals train your muscles to extract more oxygen from each unit of blood. Combined with increased cardiac output, this is the one-two punch that drives VO2 max higher.
