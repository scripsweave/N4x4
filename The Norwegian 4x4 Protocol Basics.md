# The Norwegian 4x4 Protocol: VO2 Max Training

The **Norwegian 4x4 protocol** is a high-intensity interval training (HIIT) method designed to improve cardiovascular capacity (VO2 Max).

**Medical Disclaimer:** High-intensity exercise places significant stress on the cardiovascular system. Consult with a healthcare professional before beginning this or any new exercise program, especially if there are pre-existing health conditions or if one has been sedentary.

---

## 🏃 The Core Protocol
*   **Warm-up:** 5 minutes (Gradually increasing to a moderate pace)
*   **The Work:** 4 minutes (85–95% Max HR / High intensity)
*   **The Rest:** 3 minutes (60–70% Max HR / Active recovery)
*   **Repeat:** 4 times total
*   **Cool-down:** 5 minutes (Light movement)

---

## 💡 Guidelines for Implementation

### **For Beginners**
*   **Intensity Check:** During work sets, breathing should be heavy enough that speaking full sentences is difficult.
*   **Progression:** Starting with 2 intervals and gradually increasing to 4 over several weeks allows for safer adaptation.
*   **Low Impact Options:** Using a stationary bike or elliptical can reduce joint impact during the initial phases.

### **Advanced Considerations**
*   **Heart Rate Monitoring:** Utilizing a heart rate monitor helps ensure the effort stays within the 85-95% Max HR range.
*   **Recovery:** High-intensity sessions require significant recovery time. Aim for 48–72 hours between 4x4 sessions.
*   **Nutrition:** Adequate carbohydrate intake prior to training supports the high-intensity nature of the intervals.

---

## 🛠️ Modality Guide


| Method          | The "Work" Phase (4 Mins)       | The "Rest" Phase (3 Mins) |
| :-------------- | :------------------------------ | :------------------------ |
| **Treadmill**   | 3–5% Incline; steady hard run.  | Slow walk.                |
| **Outdoor Run** | Steady uphill or flat hard run. | Light jog or brisk walk.  |
| **Rowing**      | Powerful, consistent strokes.   | Very light rowing.        |
| **Cycling**     | High resistance/cadence.        | Slow pedaling.            |
